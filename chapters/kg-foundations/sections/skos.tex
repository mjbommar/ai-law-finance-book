\section{SKOS for Taxonomies and Thesauri}
\label{sec:kg-foundations-skos}

SKOS models controlled vocabularies with concepts, preferred/alternate labels, and hierarchical and mapping relations. It is ideal for legal/financial taxonomies, harmonizing synonyms, acronyms, and crosswalks. \autocite{w3c-skos-reference}

\begin{definitionbox}[title={When to Use SKOS vs. OWL}]
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{SKOS}: curated terms and relationships among concepts; lightweight semantics.
  \item \textbf{OWL}: domain ontology with class axioms, property characteristics, and reasoning.
\end{itemize}
\end{definitionbox}
